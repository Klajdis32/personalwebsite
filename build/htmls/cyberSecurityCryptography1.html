    Solution:

    DES is vulnerable to brute-force attacks due to its short key length (56 bits). Knowing part of the key (e.g., 16 or 40 bits) significantly reduces the time required for decryption, proving that security depends on the full key length and its secrecy. Additionally, ECB mode introduces vulnerabilities, as identical plaintext blocks lead to identical ciphertext blocks, further facilitating analysis.
